Comparison: Your ERP vs SAP ERP
Your current system is a small Flask-based ERP scaffold with:

CRM: leads, tickets, quotations, notes
Finance: billing, expenses, taxes, accounts summary
HRM: employees, attendance, payroll, leave
Core operations: inventory, sales, purchases

SAP ERP is a full enterprise suite with many integrated modules, including:

Financial Accounting (FI)
Controlling (CO)
Materials Management (MM)
Sales and Distribution (SD)
Production Planning (PP)
Human Capital Management (HCM)
Warehouse Management (WM)
Asset Management (EAM)
Quality Management (QM)
Workflow, approvals, audit, and multi-company consolidation

Recommended Priorities for Your App
Deepen Finance first: GL + AP/AR will unlock real ERP value.
Formalize Procurement: PO + GRN + vendor workflows.
Extend Inventory to Warehouse Operations: bin locations, transfers, reservations.
Add Workflow/Approval Engine: critical for enterprise process control.
Add better Reporting and Alerts: make the ERP actionable.
What to Avoid for Now
These are SAP-scale features that are probably too large for the current scope:

Full production planning / advanced MRP
Quality management modules
Full HCM suite with talent management
SAP-style enterprise consolidation
Highly complex tax/legal compliance features
Conclusion
Yes — you can definitely add many SAP-style functionalities incrementally. The best path is:

keep the existing CRM/Finance/HRM core,
strengthen inventory/warehouse and procurement,
add workflow/approval and reporting,
then move toward multi-company and API integration.
